BIZAK DESIGN BRIEF — for feeding to a design LLM
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

This document is the complete, self-contained design language for Bizak. It
covers both the marketing site (bizakerp.com) and the product app
(system.bizakerp.com). Read it end-to-end before designing anything.


───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
1. WHAT BIZAK IS
───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

Bizak ERP — "The Operating System for Modern Business." A unified cloud ERP
that replaces the spreadsheet + 6-SaaS-subs stack most SMEs accumulate.

Always sold as a platform, never a point-tool. Even module pages (Finance,
Inventory, Sales, etc.) frame their module as a piece of a unified whole.

Audience. Finance director / ops lead / COO at a ~50-person manufacturing,
distribution, services or retail company. They've outgrown spreadsheets but
don't want NetSuite/SAP. They want real-time visibility, not feature lists.

Product surface:
  • Core Modules: Financial Management, Sales & CRM, Purchasing, Inventory &
    Warehouse, Manufacturing, Projects & Job Costing, Sales Force Management,
    Point of Sale.
  • Platform Capabilities: Dashboards & Reporting, Workflow Automation,
    Integrations, Multi-company & Multi-branch, Document Management.
  • By-Industry: Manufacturing, Distribution & Logistics, Professional
    Services, Retail & E-Commerce.


───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
2. THE SIX PRODUCT NARRATIVES (every page leans on a subset)
───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

1. Real-time, not month-end — every transaction posts the moment it happens.
   Phrases: "Live", "As of now", "Month-end in hours, not weeks."

2. Single source of truth — one DB, every module, no integration tax.
   Phrases: "One ledger, every module", "Connected by design."

3. No manual coding — events auto-post journals. Shipment → COGS / Inventory;
   Invoice → AR / Revenue + Tax. Phrases: "Auto-posted", "0 manual entries."

4. Full audit trail — every figure resolves to its source transaction.
   Phrases: "Click any number to see its source", "Compliance-ready."

5. Multi-entity & multi-currency native — designed from day 1 for global
   subsidiaries. Phrases: "Auto-consolidated", "FX translation applied."

6. Replaces the spreadsheet stack — the honest foil is "Excel + QuickBooks +
   Trello + 4 SaaS subs", not NetSuite. Phrases: "Beyond Excel."


───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
3. BRAND VOICE
───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

• Confident, terse, technical-but-accessible. Short sentences. Specific
  numbers. No hedge words ("could", "may help to", "potentially").
• Outcomes over features. "60% faster month-end" beats "powerful close
  engine." Anchor numbers ("99%" of what? — always say).
• Show, don't tell. A live-streaming journal panel says "real-time" louder
  than the word "real-time." Prefer mockups + data over adjectives.
• No AI-magic clichés. Refer to AI features concretely ("AI-driven bank
  statement matching"), never as "AI-powered platform" wash.
• Never call Bizak a "tool" or "app" — it's a platform / operating system.
• If a sentence works equally well for Salesforce/NetSuite/HubSpot, rewrite.

Canonical statistics (reuse rather than invent):
  50,000+ companies powered by Bizak
  60%     faster month-end close
  40%     reduction in manual data-entry errors
  100%    audit compliance
  87.4%   average OEE (manufacturing)
  96.2%   on-time delivery
  99.8%   consolidation accuracy
  247     journal entries auto-posted in a day (live-demo number)


───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
4. VISUAL SIGNATURE
───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

1. Palette: paper-cream base + olive dark + lime accent + pistachio
   secondary. No sage, no gradients, ever.

2. Alternating section backgrounds (marketing only). Consecutive content
   sections alternate paper #FCFCF7 and warm cream #efefe9. Dark sections
   (#1A2D20 olive) are sparing showcase blocks.

3. Hero pattern (marketing only). Centered copy on warm-cream surface;
   pistachio confetti pill above h1; two pill CTAs; optional dark olive
   "HeroCanvas" panel below with floating cards. Dot-grid texture overlay,
   never a gradient.

4. Live data over static screenshots. Mocks show flowing data — pulsing
   "Live" pills, streaming journal rows, OEE gauges, auto-posting feeds.
   This is the visual language of "real-time."

5. Bento layouts. Asymmetric column spans, not uniform N-column grids.

6. DotGrid for depth on dark. Texture comes from a 1px grid overlay in
   rgba(252,252,247,0.05). Never gradients.

7. Single font: Inter. Everywhere — body, headings, IDs, SKUs, numerical
   readouts. Differentiate with letter-spacing or weight, never a font swap.
   Default heading weight is 500 (calmer than 700).

8. Single accent: lime #d3f969. Pistachio #DBE9B8 is the soft secondary.
   Everything else is paper / olive / muted-grey.

9. Pills carry meaning. Pill variants and status chips are semantic
   functional labels, not decoration.

───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
6. THE `tone` PROP CONVENTION
───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

`tone` describes the SURFACE the element sits on, not its text colour.

  tone="light"   (default)  element sits on a light surface → dark text/paint
  tone="dark"               element sits on a dark surface → paper text/paint
  tone="a"  (Section)       paper #FCFCF7
  tone="b"  (Section)       warm cream #efefe9
  tone="dark" (Section)     olive #1A2D20

Applied identically across Section, Heading, Eyebrow, SectionHead, DataRow,
EntityRow, JournalRow, MiniBars, ModuleListItem, StatTile, StripeBar,
Marquee.

───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
9. CLOSING CTA — OWNED BY <Footer cta={…}>, NEVER A PAGE SECTION
───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

The "Take full control of…" moment is rendered by Footer via its `cta` prop.
Pages override the default by passing `cta` from their *PageLayout in
routes.tsx. Never add a separate <Section tone="dark"> closing-CTA block
above <Footer> — that's the legacy pattern.

  <Footer cta={{
    title: "Take full control of your sales operations.",
    titleMuted: "Quote to cash in hours, not days.",
    description: "One order, auto-posted journals, live margin — and a full audit trail.",
    primaryLabel: "Start free trial",
    primaryHref: "https://system.bizakerp.com/account/self-register",
    secondaryLabel: "Talk to sales",
    secondaryHref: "/contact",
  }}/>


───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
10. SECTION RHYTHM — WHAT'S FIXED vs FREE (MARKETING PAGES)
───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

FIXED across every page (the design LANGUAGE):
  • Palette + tokens + Inter type scale from theme.css
  • bz/ primitive vocabulary — composition over forks
  • Alternating tone="a" / tone="b" between consecutive content sections
  • Dark sections sparing
  • Hero shell exactly as above
  • Closing CTA via <Footer cta={…}>
  • Clean, minimalistic, no gradients, single lime accent per surface

FREE per page (the design CHOICES — invent these):
  • Which sections, in what order
  • What each section IS (bento grid, step row, custom dashboard mock,
    streaming-data panel, something invented)
  • The page-specific mocks inside HeroCanvas and showcase blocks
  • Which narratives (§2) and stats (§3) this page leans on

Rule of thumb: if two redesigned pages feel like the same page with
different copy, the structure is too rigid.

A known-good rhythm (HomePage — ONE valid composition, not a spec):
  1. Hero (tone="b", pad="hero") — centered copy + 2 pills + HeroCanvas + logo Marquee
  2. How-it-works (tone="a") — SectionHead + 3× StepCard + FlagsRow
  3. Platform/showcase (tone="b") — SectionHead with actions + custom dashboard + BentoGrid cols=4
  4. Exclusive/capabilities (tone="a") — SectionHead + BentoGrid cols=3 + BigCard marquee feature
  5. Testimonials (tone="b") — Carousel + 3× StatTile column
  6. FAQ (tone="a") — 2-col: dark olive intro panel (DotGrid + dark SectionHead + 2 pills) left, Accordion right


───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
11. HARD RULES (NON-NEGOTIABLE)
───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

 1. Inter only, everywhere. No Hedvig, Manrope, Poppins, font-mono,
    font-family: monospace. IDs, SKUs, step numbers all stay Inter —
    differentiate with letter-spacing, weight, or color, never a font swap.

 2. Lucide icons only. Import lucide-react directly. No @mui/icons-material.
    No <span className="material-symbols-outlined">. No per-file SVG dictionary.

 3. No gradients. Period. No linear-gradient / radial-gradient /
    conic-gradient for cards, buttons, glows, dividers, badges, hero
    backgrounds, anything. Depth = flat fills + shadows + DotGrid.

 4. className over inline style. The only legitimate inline `style` is
    (a) hero spacing (marginBottom: 28/36), (b) genuinely dynamic values
    (width: ${pct}%). Never onMouseEnter/onMouseLeave style mutations —
    use hover: utilities or :hover on the primitive's CSS class.

 5. No per-file color objects. No `const C = {...}` at the top of a page
    file. Use tokens (var(--bz-fire)) or Tailwind utilities (text-bz-fire).

 6. Pages stay small. Composition + data arrays. ~150–400 lines TSX. Any
    section past ~80 lines of JSX = extract a primitive.

 7. Scope before write. New global primitives go in bz/. Never add to
    marketing/ — that folder is being deleted in Phase 4.

 8. Delegate to atoms — never reinvent. A raw <button>/<a>/styled <div>
    whose shape mirrors a primitive must delegate to that primitive. If the
    visual shape isn't expressible by an existing variant, add the variant
    on the primitive first, then delegate.

 9. Closing CTA goes in <Footer cta={…}> — never a page-level dark section.

10. Header is non-fixed. No paddingTop: 76, pt-[76px], mt-[76px] — legacy shims.

11. Sections alternate tone="a"/tone="b" between consecutive content blocks.

12. Mobile responsive from 375px. Every grid-cols-N (N ≥ 2) needs a
    grid-cols-1 mobile fallback. No fixed pixel heights on hero panels.
    Decorative sidebars hidden md:flex. Dense tables wrapped in
    overflow-x-auto + min-w-[…].

13. *PageLayout outer <div> MUST have className="bz-page". That class
    carries overflow-x: clip + text-wrap: balance — the canonical
    mobile-overflow guard.

14. No per-page <style>{@media …}</style> blocks. Responsive logic lives
    in the primitive that owns the layout.


───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
12. APPLYING THIS LANGUAGE TO PRODUCT APP SCREENS (system.bizakerp.com)
───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

The marketing primitives (Section, BentoGrid, HeroCanvas, BadgeGreen,
StepCard, BigCard) are for marketing rhythm. They DO NOT fit data-table
pages, transaction-detail pages, or entry forms. The following carries
over to the product app; the rest is reinvented.

CARRIES OVER (the design language is global):
  • Palette: paper #FCFCF7 base, olive #1A2D20 dark surfaces, lime #d3f969
    accent, pistachio #DBE9B8 soft secondary. Same hex values, same usage.
  • Inter only, with the same scale and tracking tokens.
  • No gradients, no exceptions.
  • Lucide icons only.
  • Radii: rounded-bz-md (10) for cards/inputs, rounded-bz-lg (12) for
    panels, rounded-bz-pill for status chips.
  • <StatusChip variant="live|posted|neutral"> for inline status (extend
    with more semantic variants per module: draft, confirmed, shipped,
    invoiced, paid, void, overdue).
  • <Pill variant="dark|light|leaf|accent"> for action buttons. Primary
    actions use variant="dark"; secondary use variant="light" or
    variant="ghost".
  • <DotGrid tone="dark"> for any dark surface in the app (sidebar,
    drawer headers, KPI strip backgrounds).
  • Mono-style numerical readouts use Inter with tabular-nums +
    letter-spacing -0.01em + weight 500. Never a mono font.
  • Lime accent is sparing — used ONLY for the single most important
    affordance on screen (the primary CTA, an active state, a positive
    delta). Never for body chrome.

DOES NOT CARRY OVER:
  • Section alternation tone="a"/tone="b" — app screens are continuous
    surfaces, not rhythmic marketing sections.
  • The hero shell — app screens have a page header bar, not a hero.
  • Closing CTA via Footer — app screens don't have a marketing footer.
  • BentoGrid composition for storytelling — app screens use functional
    layouts (data tables, two-column form+sidebar, three-pane workbench).
  • BadgeGreen confetti pill — used only for marketing hero.

APP SCREEN ANATOMY (the recurring shapes):

  PAGE HEADER BAR (fixed top of content area, NOT the marketing header)
    • Left: breadcrumb (Module › Submodule › Page) + page title (h2 size,
      weight 500, --bz-text).
    • Right: action cluster — <Pill variant="dark">New X</Pill> primary,
      plus filter/export/settings icon-buttons.
    • Bottom border: 1px border-bz-line-soft.
    • Padding: 24px horizontal, 20px vertical.

  KPI STRIP (optional, under the page header)
    • Row of 3–5 stat tiles on bg-bz-section-b or bg-bz-paper-warm.
    • Each tile: meta-label (text-bz-text-muted, 11px, uppercase tracking
      0.18em), big number (Inter 500, 26px, tabular-nums), delta chip
      using StatusChip variant.

  DATA TABLE (the list-view core)
    • Outer wrapper: rounded-bz-lg border border-bz-line-soft bg-bz-surface.
    • Toolbar row at top: search input (left), filter chips, view density,
      column settings, export, bulk-action menu.
    • Table headers: bg-bz-paper-warm, text-bz-text-muted, 11.5px,
      uppercase tracking 0.12em, weight 500, sortable arrow icons.
    • Rows: 56–64px height, separator border-bz-line-soft, hover
      bg-bz-paper-warm/60, click → detail view.
    • Status cells use <StatusChip>. Money cells right-aligned, tabular-nums,
      slightly darker than --bz-text.
    • Selected row: bg-bz-fire-soft border-l-2 border-bz-fire.
    • Pagination footer: row count left, page controls right.

  DETAIL VIEW (the transaction view)
    • Two columns: main content (8/12) + sidebar (4/12) on desktop;
      stacks on mobile.
    • Header card at top: doc# + status chip + primary actions on the
      right (Confirm / Cancel / More).
    • Main: tabs (Overview, Items, Shipments, Invoices, Activity), each
      tab a panel with rounded-bz-lg border on bg-bz-surface.
    • Sidebar: meta panels — Customer, Addresses, Totals, Audit Trail.
      Each panel is a small card with section title + key/value DataRows.
    • Activity / Audit panel uses JournalRow-style entries: actor avatar
      circle + "Sara confirmed SO-1041" + relative timestamp + drilldown
      chevron. The audit trail is the embodiment of narrative #4.

  CREATE/EDIT FORM (the entry form)
    • Single column, max-w-[920px], left-aligned (NOT centered).
    • Sticky form header at top: title (e.g. "New Sales Order") + Save
      Draft (variant="light") + Confirm (variant="dark") on the right.
    • Sections grouped as cards: rounded-bz-lg border-bz-line-soft
      bg-bz-surface, 28px internal padding, 20px gap between sections.
    • Each section has a small icon-badge (24px square,
      rounded-bz-md, bg-bz-paper-warm) + section title + helper text
      underneath.
    • Fields: label above input. Labels are 12.5px text-bz-text-muted
      uppercase tracking-0.10em. Inputs: 40px height, rounded-bz-md,
      border-bz-line, bg-bz-surface, 14px Inter, focus ring is
      ring-2 ring-bz-fire/40.
    • Line-items grid (for an order): inline table inside the form
      section, with add-row at the bottom. Quantity / Rate / Tax / Total
      columns auto-compute the line total; running total at the bottom.
    • Validation: error text below the field, 12px text-[#B7393A] (red
      not yet tokenized — propose --bz-danger #B7393A).
    • Sticky totals card at the bottom-right of long forms.

  EMPTY STATE
    • Centered, illustration-less. Lucide icon at 40px in text-bz-text-soft,
      h3-sized title, paragraph subtext, primary <Pill> CTA.

  SIDE DRAWER (for quick-create / row preview)
    • Slides from the right, 480–560px wide.
    • Header bar with title + close X. Body uses the same form patterns
      as the full create view but compressed.

  COMMAND PALETTE (Cmd-K)
    • Floating panel, 640px wide, rounded-bz-xl border-bz-line-soft
      bg-bz-surface shadow-[0_20px_60px_rgba(15,20,17,0.18)].

THE PRODUCT APP'S LEFT NAV (persistent shell)
  • Width: 240px expanded / 64px collapsed.
  • bg-bz-olive, text-bz-text-on-dark, DotGrid overlay at 0.05 opacity.
  • Sections: Workspace switcher (top) → primary modules (Finance, Sales,
    Inventory, Manufacturing, Projects) → admin (Settings, Users) at bottom.
  • Active item: bg-white/[0.06] + 2px left border-bz-fire + lime icon.
  • Module group headers: 10.5px text-white/[0.55] uppercase tracking 0.18em.

THE PRODUCT APP'S TOP BAR
  • 56px tall, bg-bz-surface, border-b border-bz-line-soft.
  • Left: workspace name + branch/entity selector chip.
  • Center: command-palette trigger (Search… ⌘K) in a 360px input pill.
  • Right: notifications icon + help icon + user avatar.


───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
13. THE ONE MENTAL MODEL
───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

Pages are composition + data. Primitives own colour, spacing, type, hover,
breakpoints. Tokens own all values. Header/footer + closing CTA are owned
by the route layout. Hero is one shape; marketing section structure is
freely invented within a fixed design language. Real-time live data over
static screenshots. Single lime accent per surface. Inter and only Inter.
No gradients, no exceptions.

That's the system. Anything that violates it isn't Bizak.

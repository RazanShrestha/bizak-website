BIZAK ERP — MOBILE APP LOGIN SCREEN — DESIGN PROMPT
======================================================

Feed this brief to your design LLM. It produces a high-fidelity mockup of the
Bizak mobile-app login flow that matches the Bizak website's design system
exactly. Read every section before designing — the design language is
non-negotiable; only the marked "design choices" are open.


------------------------------------------------------------------------
1. ROLE & TASK
------------------------------------------------------------------------

You are designing the LOGIN SCREEN for the Bizak ERP mobile app (iOS/Android).
It is a TWO-STEP flow, one full screen per step:

  Step 1 — Email      (enter work email → Continue)
  Step 2 — Password   (enter password → Log in)

The flow must feel minimal, calm, and unmistakably "Bizak" — the same design
language as the Bizak marketing website (bizakerp.com). The Bizak logo is the
hero visual of the screen; use it to carry the design, not as a small mark
in a corner.

Deliver high-fidelity mockups of BOTH steps at mobile size (see §7).


------------------------------------------------------------------------
2. WHAT BIZAK IS (so the tone is right)
------------------------------------------------------------------------

Bizak ERP — "The Operating System for Modern Business." A unified cloud ERP
that small-to-mid-market companies run their finance, sales, inventory,
manufacturing and operations on, replacing a stack of spreadsheets and
disconnected SaaS. Early-stage, focused on South Asia.

Voice: confident, terse, technical-but-accessible. Short sentences. No
marketing hedge, no AI-magic clichés, no vanity metrics. Outcomes over
features. The login screen should feel like the calm front door to a serious
operating system — not a flashy consumer app.


------------------------------------------------------------------------
3. REFERENCE & INTENT
------------------------------------------------------------------------

The structural reference is the Ramp app's login screen — a "step screen":
a short headline at the top, a single hero visual in the middle, and ONE
input field + ONE primary button anchored at the bottom. Generous empty
space. One decision per screen.

TAKE from the reference:
  • The step structure — one field + one button per screen, nothing else.
  • The bottom-anchored form block.
  • The single, confident hero visual that owns the middle of the screen.
  • The generous, unhurried whitespace.

IGNORE from the reference:
  • Its all-dark palette — Bizak's login is a LIGHT paper-cream screen.
  • Its physical-card product shot — Bizak has no card; the hero visual is a
    logo-card built from the Bizak brand mark (see §6).
  • Its disabled-looking grey button — Bizak's primary button is solid and
    confident.

The result must look like a Bizak product, not a Ramp clone.


------------------------------------------------------------------------
4. DESIGN LANGUAGE — NON-NEGOTIABLE
------------------------------------------------------------------------

4.1 PALETTE  (use these exact hex values — do not invent shades)

  Surfaces
    Paper (page bg)        #FCFCF7   ← the login screen background
    Paper-warm             #F4F4ED   ← input fill / subtle inset blocks
    Surface (white card)   #FFFFFF
    Olive (dark)           #1A2D20   ← the hero logo-card
    Olive-soft             #243A2D
    Deep (button fill)     #0F1411   ← primary button fill

  Accent
    Lime / "fire"          #d3f969   ← THE single pop colour. The logomark,
                                       one focus accent. Use sparingly.
    Lime-soft              rgba(211,249,105,0.10)
    Lime-mid               rgba(211,249,105,0.20)
    Pistachio / "leaf"     #DBE9B8   ← soft secondary accent (e.g. a "Secure"
                                       badge tint)

  Text
    Text (primary)         #1A1D19
    Text-muted             #6E7466   ← labels, helper copy, secondary text
    Text-soft              #A2A296   ← the muted half of a two-tone heading
    Text-on-dark           #FCFCF7   ← any text/mark on the olive card
    Text-on-dark-muted     rgba(252,252,247,0.62)

  Lines
    Line                   #D8D9D3   ← input borders, dividers
    Line-soft              #E5E5E0

4.2 TYPOGRAPHY
  • ONE font family: Inter. End to end. No exceptions, no second family,
    no monospace. Differentiate with weight / size / letter-spacing only.
  • Weights available: 300, 400, 500, 600, 700. Headings use weight 500
    (the brand is calm, not loud — do NOT use 700 for the headline).
  • Scale for this screen:
      Headline       28–32px, weight 500, line-height ~1.15, tracking -0.02em
      Sub / helper   14–15px, weight 400, text-muted, line-height ~1.6
      Field label    13px,    weight 500, text-muted
      Input text     16px     (never below 16px — prevents mobile zoom)
      Button label   15–16px, weight 500
      Small links    13–14px, weight 500
      Eyebrow (opt)  12px,    weight 600, UPPERCASE, tracking 0.22em

4.3 RADII   (use this scale — not generic "rounded-lg")
    sm 6 · md 10 · lg 12 · xl 18 · 2xl 22 · 3xl 28 · pill 9999
  • Input fields:    lg (12px)
  • Hero logo-card:  2xl–3xl (22–28px)
  • Primary button:  pill (9999) — fully rounded, full-width
  • Small badge:     pill

4.4 HARD RULES — breaking any of these fails the brief
    1. NO GRADIENTS anywhere. Not on backgrounds, cards, buttons, glows,
       borders. Flat fills only. Depth comes from soft shadows, flat olive,
       and 1px line texture — never a gradient.
    2. ONE font: Inter. ONE accent: lime #d3f969, used sparingly.
    3. Clean and minimal. One decision per screen. Empty space is the design
       — do not fill it with decorative chrome.
    4. The screen background is flat paper-cream #FCFCF7. No patterned bg.
    5. Soft, restrained shadows only (for the logo-card and the button).
       No hard drop shadows, no neon glows.


------------------------------------------------------------------------
5. THE BIZAK LOGO
------------------------------------------------------------------------

The Bizak brand mark has two parts:
  • The SYMBOL — an organic, curved "leaf/blade" mark made of two nested
    arc-shaped strokes. It reads as motion and growth. In brand lime green.
  • The WORDMARK — "bizak", lowercase, in near-black.

Logo lockups exist in: all-black, all-white, all-green/lime, dark-mode, and
a primary (lime symbol + dark wordmark) version — in horizontal and vertical
layouts.

USE THE LOGO AS THE HERO VISUAL — this is the core instruction.
  • On the olive logo-card (§6): use the lime/white logo lockup so the mark
    glows on the dark olive.
  • The logo's own geometry is curved concentric arcs — echo that geometry
    in the card's texture (see §6). This is the "use the logo to enhance
    the design" move: the texture is derived from the mark itself.
  • Do NOT stretch, recolour outside the palette, add effects to, or crop
    the logo. Keep clear space around it.


------------------------------------------------------------------------
6. THE HERO VISUAL — THE "LOGO-CARD"
------------------------------------------------------------------------

The middle of the screen is owned by a single hero visual: a LOGO-CARD.

  • A rounded rectangle card, radius 22–28px, filled flat olive #1A2D20.
  • Soft, deep, diffuse shadow beneath it — it floats gently on the
    paper-cream screen. (Shadow, NOT a glow, NOT a gradient.)
  • Centered on the card: the Bizak logomark in lime #d3f969 (symbol, or
    symbol + lowercase "bizak" wordmark in paper-white). Large and confident.
  • TEXTURE — echo the logomark's own curved-arc geometry: faint 1px
    concentric arc strokes radiating across the olive card, in
    rgba(252,252,247,0.06–0.10). Flat strokes only. This is what makes the
    card feel crafted, and ties it back to the brand mark. (A subtle 1px
    dot-grid is an acceptable alternative texture.)
  • RECOMMENDED: tilt the card a gentle 4–6° — a calm, deliberate lean that
    gives the screen life (a nod to the reference). Upright with no tilt is
    the acceptable minimal fallback. Either way it stays composed, not playful.
  • Optional, tiny: one line of paper-white-muted micro-copy on the card,
    e.g. "The Operating System for Modern Business." Keep it whisper-quiet
    or omit it. The mark is the message.

[DESIGN CHOICE — open] The exact card proportion, the tilt amount, whether
the wordmark joins the symbol, and the arc-texture density are yours to
tune. Keep it minimal: the card should feel like one clean object.

Step 1 and Step 2 share the SAME logo-card so the flow feels continuous.


------------------------------------------------------------------------
7. SCREEN ARCHITECTURE  (applies to both steps)
------------------------------------------------------------------------

Frame: mobile, 375 × 812pt baseline. Respect top (~59px) and bottom (~34px)
safe areas. Design for 375px first; it must also hold up to ~430px wide.

Vertical zones, top → bottom:

  ┌─────────────────────────────────────┐
  │ (status bar — system)               │
  │ A. TOP BAR                          │  back arrow (Step 2 only) +
  │                                     │  thin 2-segment step indicator
  │ B. HEADLINE BLOCK                   │  short headline + one helper line
  │                                     │
  │ C. HERO LOGO-CARD                   │  the §6 visual, centered
  │     (flexible space above + below)  │
  │                                     │
  │ D. FORM BLOCK  (anchored low)       │  one field + primary button
  │ E. SECONDARY LINKS                  │  small text links
  │ (home indicator — system)           │
  └─────────────────────────────────────┘

  • A — TOP BAR. A thin 2-segment progress indicator (each segment a small
    rounded bar): Step 1 = first segment filled lime/olive, second is
    line-soft; Step 2 = both filled. On Step 2 only, a back arrow (lucide
    "arrow-left", text colour) sits at the left. Minimal — no title text here.
  • B — HEADLINE. A short headline (weight 500) + one muted helper line.
    Left-aligned. (Centered is an acceptable alternative — pick one and keep
    it consistent across both steps.)
  • C — HERO LOGO-CARD. The §6 visual. It absorbs the flexible vertical
    space between the headline and the form.
  • D — FORM BLOCK. Anchored toward the bottom (like the reference). One
    input field, then the full-width primary button directly below it.
  • E — SECONDARY LINKS. Small, quiet text links beneath the button.

  Horizontal page padding: 24px left/right.
  Vertical rhythm: generous — ~28–40px between zones. Let it breathe.

KEYBOARD BEHAVIOUR: when the keyboard opens, the FORM BLOCK (D) and the
primary button stay visible directly above the keyboard. The logo-card (C)
may shrink or scroll up gracefully — the field and button must never be
hidden behind the keyboard.


------------------------------------------------------------------------
8. STEP 1 — EMAIL
------------------------------------------------------------------------

  A. Top bar:   2-segment indicator, segment 1 filled. No back arrow.
  B. Headline:  "One login. Your whole business."
     Helper:    "Sign in to your Bizak workspace."
     (Alternative headlines, pick the calmest: "Welcome to Bizak." /
      "Sign in to Bizak.")
  C. Logo-card: the §6 hero visual.
  D. Form:
       • Field — label "Work email", type=email, placeholder e.g.
         "you@company.com". Keyboard = email. Autofocus optional.
       • Primary button — label "Continue", full-width pill, with a small
         right-arrow (→) at the end of the label. Deep #0F1411 fill,
         paper-white text. (See §10 for button spec.)
  E. Secondary links (small, centered or left, muted):
       • "New to Bizak?  Get started" — "Get started" is the lime/olive
         emphasised link, opens self-registration.

Behaviour note (for state mockups): button is enabled once the email looks
valid; tapping Continue advances to Step 2.


------------------------------------------------------------------------
9. STEP 2 — PASSWORD
------------------------------------------------------------------------

  A. Top bar:   back arrow at left + 2-segment indicator, both filled.
  B. Headline:  "Welcome back."
     Helper:    show the email entered in Step 1 as a small, quiet
                identity row beneath the headline — the email in text
                colour + a tiny "Change" link (lucide "pencil" or a text
                link) to return to Step 1. This confirms context without a
                second field.
  C. Logo-card: the SAME §6 hero visual as Step 1 (continuity).
  D. Form:
       • Field — label "Password", type=password, with a show/hide eye
         toggle (lucide "eye" / "eye-off") inside the field on the right.
       • Primary button — label "Log in", full-width pill, Deep #0F1411
         fill, paper-white text. (No arrow on the final action, or a small
         → — designer's choice; keep it consistent if used.)
  E. Secondary links (small, muted):
       • "Forgot password?" — quiet text link.


------------------------------------------------------------------------
10. COMPONENTS & STATES  (mock the key states)
------------------------------------------------------------------------

INPUT FIELD
  • Shape: full-width, height 52–56px, radius 12px (lg).
  • Fill: paper-warm #F4F4ED  (or white #FFFFFF — pick one, stay consistent).
  • Border: 1px line #D8D9D3.
  • Label: 13px, weight 500, text-muted #6E7466 — sitting just above the
    field. (A floating label that rests as placeholder and animates up on
    focus is an acceptable, more minimal alternative.)
  • Input text: 16px, text #1A1D19.
  • States to show:
      – Default:  as above.
      – Focus:    border darkens to olive #1A2D20 (1.5px) + a soft 3px
                  lime-soft rgba(211,249,105,0.10) ring. This is the ONE
                  place the lime accent appears in the form.
      – Filled:   value in full text colour.
      – Error:    border + helper text in a restrained, muted brick-red
                  (quiet, not alarming) with a one-line message below the
                  field, e.g. "Enter a valid email" / "Incorrect password."
                  Keep it calm — no shouting red.

PRIMARY BUTTON
  • Full-width, height 52–56px, radius pill (9999).
  • Fill: Deep #0F1411 (flat). Label: paper-white #FCFCF7, 15–16px weight 500.
  • Optional small arrow icon (lucide "arrow-right") trailing the label.
  • States to show:
      – Default:  solid deep fill, confident.
      – Pressed:  very slight darken / scale-down — subtle.
      – Loading:  label replaced by a small spinner; fill unchanged (it is
                  NOT greyed out).
      – Disabled: fill drops to a muted olive/grey, text text-soft — but
                  prefer to keep it enabled and validate on tap rather than
                  showing a dead grey button.

STEP INDICATOR
  • Two small rounded segment bars, ~4px tall, in the top bar.
  • Filled segment: olive #1A2D20 or lime #d3f969 (pick one). Empty
    segment: line-soft #E5E5E0.

SECONDARY LINKS
  • 13–14px, weight 500. Resting colour text-muted #6E7466; the
    emphasised word ("Get started") in text #1A1D19 or olive. Underline on
    press only. No buttons — plain text links.

OPTIONAL "SECURE" BADGE
  • A small pill badge (pistachio leaf #DBE9B8 fill, dark text, lucide
    "lock" or "shield-check" icon), e.g. "Encrypted & secure", placed
    quietly near the form or footer. Optional — only if it does not add
    clutter. Minimalism wins ties.


------------------------------------------------------------------------
11. MOTION / TRANSITION  (describe, for the dev handoff)
------------------------------------------------------------------------

  • Step 1 → Step 2: a calm horizontal slide (new screen in from the right)
    OR a soft cross-fade. The logo-card stays put / morphs in place so the
    flow feels like one continuous surface, not two unrelated screens.
  • Field focus, button press: short, ease-out, ~150–200ms. Calm, never
    bouncy.
  • No parallax, no confetti, no flashy entrance animation.


------------------------------------------------------------------------
12. ACCESSIBILITY
------------------------------------------------------------------------

  • Input text ≥ 16px (prevents mobile auto-zoom).
  • All tap targets ≥ 48 × 48px.
  • Text/background contrast: body text on paper, and paper-white on olive,
    must pass WCAG AA.
  • The lime accent is decorative/focus only — never the sole carrier of
    meaning (don't rely on colour alone for the error state; use text too).
  • Labels are always visible to screen readers (a floating label must keep
    an accessible name).
  • Logical focus order: field → button → secondary links.

14. DO NOT
------------------------------------------------------------------------

  ✗ No gradients — anywhere, for any reason.
  ✗ No second font, no monospace.
  ✗ No second accent colour; lime is the only pop, used sparingly.
  ✗ No dark full-screen background — the screen is paper-cream.
  ✗ No more than one input field and one primary button per step.
  ✗ No social-login row, no "or continue with" divider, no marketing
    illustration — unless explicitly added later. Keep it minimal.
  ✗ No hard drop shadows or neon glows — soft, diffuse shadows only.
  ✗ No 700-weight shouting headline — the brand is calm (weight 500).
  ✗ Do not shrink the logo to a tiny corner mark — the logo-card IS the
    hero of the screen.
  ✗ Do not clone the Ramp screenshot's colours or its physical card — take
    its STRUCTURE only.

End of brief.

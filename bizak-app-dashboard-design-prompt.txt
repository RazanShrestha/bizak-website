BIZAK ERP — MOBILE APP DASHBOARD (HOME) SCREEN — DESIGN PROMPT
================================================================

Feed this brief to your design LLM. It produces a high-fidelity mockup of the
Bizak ERP mobile-app dashboard (the Home tab).

This brief tells you WHAT the screen contains and WHICH design language to
work in — but it deliberately does NOT dictate how each component looks.
The visual design is YOURS to invent. Be creative, be premium. Stay inside
the design language; own everything else.


------------------------------------------------------------------------
1. ROLE & TASK
------------------------------------------------------------------------

Design the DASHBOARD / HOME screen of the Bizak ERP mobile app (iOS/Android)
— the first screen after login. It is a vertically scrolling feed of cards,
each card showing one slice of the business.

The result must look and feel: PREMIUM, CLEAN, PROFESSIONAL, MINIMAL. It is
the companion screen to the Bizak app login, so it must read as the same
product — the same calm, considered, high-end design language as the Bizak
marketing website (bizakerp.com).

You have full creative latitude over layout, composition, and component
design — see §6. The only things fixed are the design language (§3) and the
content (§4).


------------------------------------------------------------------------
2. WHAT BIZAK IS (so the tone is right)
------------------------------------------------------------------------

Bizak ERP — "The Operating System for Modern Business." A unified cloud ERP
that small-to-mid-market companies run their finance, sales, inventory and
field operations on. Early-stage, focused on SOUTH ASIA.

The dashboard user is a sales / ops / finance person opening the app to see,
at a glance: available cash, who owes what, how sales are trending, stock
health, and what's on their plate today.

Tone: confident, terse, professional. The dashboard shows, it does not sell.
No marketing fluff, no exclamation marks.

Regional framing — Bizak is a SOUTH ASIA product. All sample data in the
mockup must be South-Asian: currency in Rs / NPR (or ₹ / INR), a South-Asian
name in the greeting, plausible South-Asian item / customer names. No $ /
USD, no Western names.


------------------------------------------------------------------------
3. THE DESIGN LANGUAGE — THIS IS THE FIXED PART
------------------------------------------------------------------------

Work entirely within the Bizak website design language. This is the anchor;
everything visual must feel like it belongs to the same system.

PALETTE — use these colours; YOU decide where each one goes. The palette is
intentionally restrained: paper-cream + olive + a single lime accent. Choose
combinations that read premium and calm. Do not introduce colours outside
this set.

  Paper / cream      #FCFCF7   light page & card surfaces
  Paper-warm         #F4F4ED   slightly warmer cream
  Section-b (warm)   #efefe9   a warm off-white, good for the screen base
  Surface white      #FFFFFF   clean elevated card surface
  Olive (dark)       #1A2D20   the dark surface — rich, deep green
  Olive-soft         #243A2D   a lighter olive
  Deep               #0F1411   the deepest near-black green
  Lime / accent      #d3f969   THE single accent — a fresh lime. Use it
                                sparingly: it should feel like a highlight,
                                never a flood.
  Pistachio          #DBE9B8   a soft secondary accent
  Pistachio-deep     #A8C76C   a deeper pistachio
  Text               #1A1D19   primary text / numbers
  Text-muted         #6E7466   secondary text
  Text-soft          #A2A296   the quietest captions
  Text on dark       #FCFCF7   text on olive surfaces
  Line               #D8D9D3   borders / dividers
  Line-soft          #E5E5E0   hairline dividers

  For genuinely negative / "needs attention" data (overdue, a down-trend)
  the palette has no red. Use ONE restrained, desaturated warm tone (a quiet
  muted brick / terracotta) — calm, never an alarming bright red — and use
  it consistently. Do not add a second or third semantic colour beyond that.

TYPOGRAPHY
  • One font only: Inter. End to end — labels, numbers, IDs, everything.
  • Calm weights — 400 / 500 / 600. Reserve the heaviest weight for at most
    one hero number. The brand is composed, not loud; avoid 800-weight text.
  • A clear hierarchy: large calm numbers for the figures that matter, small
    tracked uppercase labels for eyebrows, quiet muted captions for support.

SHAPE & FEEL
  • Soft, considered corner radii — a small consistent radius scale (roughly
    6 / 10 / 12 / 18 / 22, plus full pills). Pick from it; stay consistent.
  • Generous whitespace. Empty space is part of the design — let it breathe.
  • Flat surfaces with hairline 1px borders; depth from at most a single
    soft, diffuse shadow. Restraint reads as premium.
  • NO GRADIENTS anywhere — backgrounds, cards, charts, fills, all flat.
  • A subtle 1px dot-grid is the only sanctioned texture, used sparingly on
    dark surfaces if at all.

THE FEELING TO HIT
  Premium, editorial, calm. Think a high-end finance product: confident
  typography, lots of air, one crisp accent, nothing shouting. If a screen
  feels busy, dense, or "dashboard-y", it has failed — simplify until it
  feels effortless.


------------------------------------------------------------------------
4. WHAT THE DASHBOARD CONTAINS
------------------------------------------------------------------------

The dashboard is a scrolling feed of section cards. Each section below is
described by WHAT it communicates — not how to build it. How each one looks,
how data is visualised, and how the cards are composed is your design call.

  • A TOP GREETING — a time-aware greeting ("Good morning", etc.) with the
    user's name, and a way to reach their profile (e.g. an avatar).

  • AVAILABLE BALANCE — the headline figure of the screen: how much cash is
    available right now. This is the moment to anchor the design — make it
    feel important.

  • RECEIVABLES & PAYABLES — money owed to the business and money it owes,
    each with its total and how much of it is overdue.

  • SALES TREND — total sales over a selectable time range, the trend
    visualised over time, and how it compares to the previous period
    (up or down).

  • TOP SALES BY ITEM — the best-selling items for a selectable range,
    shown as a share-of-total breakdown, with the total quantity sold.

  • INVENTORY — a snapshot of stock across a few states (on hand, committed,
    on order, in transit, available).

  • TODAY'S ROUTES — the user's delivery routes for today: how many are
    active vs completed. (Has an empty state: "no routes scheduled".)

  • REMINDERS — a short list of actions the user owes attention to (bills to
    approve, estimates to convert, invoices to collect), each with a count
    and an urgency. (Shown only when there are reminders.)

Each section also navigates somewhere on tap (to a detail screen). Each
section loads its data independently, so each can show a LOADING, ERROR,
or EMPTY state — design these to feel as calm and considered as the happy
path, never an afterthought.

The user can reorder the cards by dragging, and some cards are hidden
depending on their permissions — so the feed must look intentional with any
subset of cards in any order.


------------------------------------------------------------------------
5. SCREEN ARCHITECTURE (loose — refine as you see fit)
------------------------------------------------------------------------

  • Mobile, 375 × 812pt baseline; must also hold up to ~430px wide. Respect
    system safe areas top and bottom.
  • A top greeting area, a scrolling card feed below it, and the app's
    bottom tab bar at the base (Home active — a simple, clean tab bar; it is
    framing, not the focus).
  • Pull-to-refresh on the feed.

Beyond that, the composition is yours — card grouping, rhythm, where to
place the hero figure, whether any surface goes dark, how dense or airy the
feed is. Aim for premium and minimal over feature-packed.


------------------------------------------------------------------------
6. YOUR CREATIVE LATITUDE
------------------------------------------------------------------------

You decide, freely:

  • The composition and rhythm of the feed.
  • How each section card is shaped, structured, and laid out.
  • Which palette colours go where, and how the lime accent is spent.
  • Whether (and where) to use a dark olive surface for emphasis.
  • How every chart and figure is visualised.
  • Iconography, spacing, the texture of the whole thing.

The ONLY constraints: stay inside the design language (§3) and the content
(§4), and make it look genuinely premium, clean, professional and minimal.
Within that, design boldly — invent the best version of this screen.


------------------------------------------------------------------------
7. QUALITY BAR
------------------------------------------------------------------------

  • Premium first. Every choice should read considered and high-end.
  • Minimal. One clear takeaway per card. If it feels busy, cut.
  • Mobile-true. Correct and uncramped from 375px through ~430px, no
    horizontal scroll. Card interiors stay generous even when stacked.
  • Consistent. Cards, type, spacing and radii feel like one system.
  • Walk the finished screen top to bottom at 375px — if any card feels
    busy, bulky, or noisy, simplify before calling it done.


------------------------------------------------------------------------
8. DELIVERABLE
------------------------------------------------------------------------

  1. The full dashboard feed — mobile 375×812 — greeting, card feed, tab bar.
  2. A small states study — a section card shown in loading, error and empty.

All using only the palette in §3, with South-Asian sample data (Rs / NPR
amounts, a South-Asian name and item names).


------------------------------------------------------------------------
9. DO NOT
------------------------------------------------------------------------

  ✗ No gradients — anywhere, including chart fills.
  ✗ No colours outside the §3 palette. One accent (lime); one restrained
    attention tone. No bright multi-colour semantic rainbow.
  ✗ No second font, no monospace — Inter only.
  ✗ No heavy 800-weight text — keep type calm.
  ✗ No stock Material look — no Material elevation/shadow stacking, no
    bright Material spinners, no navy/slate surfaces (dark = Bizak olive).
  ✗ No busy, dense, "dashboard demo" feel — premium means restraint.
  ✗ No $ / USD, no Western sample names — this is a South Asia product.

End of brief.
